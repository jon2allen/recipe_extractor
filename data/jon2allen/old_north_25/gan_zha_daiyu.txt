### Title

gan_zha_daiyu.txt - Dry Fried Hairtail Fish

### Objectives
- Learn two methods for frying hairtail fish: dry frying and light frying.
- Master the preparation techniques for crispy, flavorful fried fish.
- Understand the importance of marinating and battering for optimal results.

### Ingredients

- Hairtail fish (amount not specified)
- Salt (amount not specified)
- MSG (amount not specified)
- Ginger (amount not specified)
- Scallions (amount not specified)
- Soy sauce (amount not specified)
- Yellow wine (amount not specified)
- Sichuan pepper powder (optional, amount not specified)
- Pepper (optional, amount not specified)
- Water (amount not specified)
- Cornstarch (amount not specified)
- All-purpose flour (amount not specified)
- Vegetable oil (amount not specified)


### Instructions

1. Clean the hairtail fish: Remove fins, internal organs, and dark blood along the spine.
2. Prepare the marinade: Mix salt, MSG, ginger, scallions, soy sauce, and yellow wine. Marinate the fish for 15-20 minutes, stirring every 5 minutes.
3. Prepare the batter: Mix cornstarch, all-purpose flour, oil, water, and a pinch of salt.
4. Cut the fish: Cut the fish into pieces about the width of a leek leaf.
5. Dry fry: Coat the fish in batter, ensuring it's not too thick. Fry in hot oil until golden brown and crispy, about 3-5 minutes, potentially in two batches.
6. Light fry: Fry the fish in hot oil until golden brown and crispy.  The fish should be fully cooked through.
7. Garnish: Garnish with optional scallions.
