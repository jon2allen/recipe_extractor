### Title

tangcu_paigu.txt - 糖醋排骨 (Sweet and Sour Spareribs)

### Objectives
- Learn to make tender and flavorful sweet and sour spareribs.
- Master the technique of creating a rich, glossy sauce.
- Achieve a perfect balance of sweet, sour, and savory flavors.
- Understand the importance of properly browning the spareribs.
- Learn how to create a beautiful, dark red color in the sauce.

### Ingredients

- 2 jin (approximately 1 kg) spareribs (排骨 páigǔ)
- 15 花椒 huājiāo (peppercorns)
- 2 大料 dàliào (star anise)
- 10 克 gè (grams) ginger slices (姜片 jiāngpiàn)
- Cooking oil (豆油 dòuyóu)
- White sugar (白糖 báitáng) 1.5 liang (approximately 75 grams)
- Rice vinegar (米醋 mǐcù) 1.5 liang (approximately 75 grams)
- Soy sauce (酱油 jiàngyóu) (amount not specified)
- Salt (盐 yán) (amount not specified)
- A small spoonful of tomato paste (番茄酱 fānqié jiàng), diluted with water
- A dash of MSG (味精 wèijīng) (optional)
- Sesame oil (香油 xiāngyóu) (a small amount)


### Instructions

1. Wash the peppercorns and star anise.
2. Prepare the spareribs: Remove the spinal bone. Cut wider parts and trim narrower parts to 1-1.5 inches in length.  Wash and scrub the spareribs thoroughly.
3. Add cooking oil to a pan and heat over high heat. Add the spareribs and stir-fry for 4 minutes until the moisture is removed and the spareribs are lightly browned. Remove from pan.
4. Add white sugar to the pan and cook until a caramel-like color is achieved.
5. Add the peppercorns, star anise, ginger, and soy sauce to the pan. Stir-fry briefly.  (Avoid burning the sugar.)
6. Add salt, 1.5 liang of white sugar, and 1.5 liang of rice vinegar. Stir until the sauce simmers.
7. Simmer for 10 minutes, then turn off the heat and let it simmer for another 10 minutes.
8. Filter the sauce to remove the spices.
9. Add a small spoonful of diluted tomato paste for color and flavor.
10. Add a dash of MSG (optional) and sesame oil.
11. Simmer until the sauce thickens and glazes the spareribs.
